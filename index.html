About Me

My full name is Bahaa Al-Din Anwar Al-Omar. I was born in Damascus in 1998 and lived in the town of Qudsaya for the first fifteen years of my life. Then, because of the war, I moved to the Dummar project area and stayed there for six years, then I moved to Latakia and I still live there until now.

"Childhood"

I was born in the Yarmouk camp neighborhood and then moved to live in Qudsaya and studied primary school at Qudsaya First School, and received the Al-Basel Certificate for Outstanding Students in the Primary School, I learned to swim at the age of seven and won two gold medals in 2007 and 2008.

"Adolescence"

I studied middle school at the Boys’ School in Qudsaya, and when I was in the eighth grade, the war began in Syria, and after obtaining a basic education certificate, I moved from Qudsaya and started working in a fast food restaurant to help my family financially.

"Young"

I had to stop my studies because of work, and then I returned at the age of eighteen and applied for the secondary certificate exam and passed it with an average of 88%, which enabled me to enter the Faculty of Mechanical and Electrical Engineering / Department of Electronics and Communications Engineering and graduated after five years with a very good grade.

"Now"

After I moved to the city of Lattakia, I began studying new technical subjects, including Microsoft Power Platform, and passed the Pl-900 exam with a score of 91%, in addition to learning web design, and helping university students complete their graduation projects in everything related to the Matlab program.
